# Interview Prep: {role} at {company}

## 1. Self-Introduction (30-60 seconds)

Use the SELF-INTRO TEMPLATE from the knowledge base as the foundation.
Tailor it for this specific role:
- Keep the career transition narrative structure
- ADD 1-2 key achievements from the resume that are most relevant to this JD
- REMOVE mentions of experiences irrelevant to this role
- End with why this specific role at this specific company excites you
- Keep it concise — this is a 30-60 second elevator pitch, not a monologue

## 2. What Do You Know About {company}?

Use COMPANY RESEARCH (from web search) to write a structured answer covering:
- What the company does (products, services, industry)
- Key facts: size, locations, recent news or milestones
- Company culture and values (from careers page, Glassdoor, JD language)
- Why this matters to you personally (connect to your background)

This should sound informed but natural, not like reading a Wikipedia article.

## 3. Why This Company?

Use the WHY-COMPANY TEMPLATE from the knowledge base as the framework:
1. Start with values and culture alignment — reference specific company values from the JD or research, explain why they resonate with you personally (with concrete examples from your experience, not empty statements)
2. Then explain what value you can bring — connect your specific skills and experiences to the role requirements, showing you understand what the role needs and why you're a good fit

Do NOT just list company facts. Every point must connect back to YOU.

## 4. STARL Stories (Key Resume Talking Points)

For each major resume bullet that has supporting evidence, write a STARL story:

- **Situation**: Set the scene AND emphasize the CHALLENGE — what made this problem hard or important? Why did it matter? The difficulty level reflects the importance of your contribution.
- **Task**: What were YOU specifically responsible for? Use "I" not "we".
- **Action**: Detailed specific steps YOU took. Include technical decisions, tools used, how you collaborated. 3-5 concrete action items, not vague summaries.
- **Result**: QUANTIFIED outcome. Numbers, percentages, time saved, users impacted. If the exact number isn't available, give a credible estimate with context.
- **Learning**: What did you learn from this experience that is directly relevant to the {role} role? Connect the learning to a JD requirement or skill.

Only create stories for bullets where you have real evidence from DEEP EXPERIENCES.
Prioritize stories that align with the JD requirements.

## 5. Likely Interview Questions & Suggested Answers

For each question from INTERVIEW QUESTIONS:
- State the question
- Provide a suggested answer framework using the candidate's actual experience
- For behavioral: use STARL format
- For technical: outline key points to cover
- For system design: suggest a high-level approach

Also include answers for these standard questions using KNOWLEDGE BASE TEMPLATES if available:
- Why transition to this role? (use why-transition template)
- What's your weakness? (use weakness template)
- How do you handle unfamiliar problems? (use open-questions template)
- Describe your work style (use work-style template)

## 6. Gap Warnings

List resume bullets that lack supporting evidence in the knowledge base.
These are areas where the candidate should prepare additional talking points independently.
